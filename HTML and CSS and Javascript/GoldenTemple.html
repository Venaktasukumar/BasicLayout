GOLDEN TEMPLE

History

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a Sikh temple located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It was built in the 16th century by the fifth Sikh guru, Guru Arjan Dev Ji, and is considered one of the holiest shrines in Sikhism.

Architecture

The Golden Temple is a stunning example of Sikh architecture, with its iconic golden dome and intricate marble work. The temple is surrounded by a large man-made lake, which adds to its beauty and tranquility.

Visit

The Golden Temple is open to visitors of all faiths and is a popular tourist destination. Visitors are required to cover their heads and remove their shoes before entering the temple complex. There are also several other attractions nearby, including the Jallianwala Bagh and the Partition Museum.
